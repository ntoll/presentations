Political Philosophers!

Asks questions like...

Is Peer-to-Peer and ubiquitous strong cryptography the answer to concerns over power & control in a digital world?

  • Myself, Holger and many others believe that it probably is (or is an important aspect of the solution).
  • Let's examine what P2P means and how this affects the political aspects of this talk.

What is Peer-to-Peer (P2P)..?

(I'll assume you know what cryptography is)

Peers of equal status (devices running appropriate software) cooperate in a loose decentralised network for mutual benefit. Peer-to-peer is the antithesis of hierarchy - where some have elevated status and power over others.

Peer-to-Peer (left) vs. Client/Server (right)

  • That red spot is a point of power and control.
  • Power and control = politics
  • Consider how this affects the WWW
The client/server architecture of the web is a fundamentally unbalanced because the server always has power over the client.
The server is a single point of failure that is also the obvious target of attacks.

(Although sometimes hierarchy is good)

(Especially when it is efficient & saves lives.)

  • In an ideal world, hierarchy is best when the obvious skill, knowledge and capabilities of an entity are acknowledged to bring about greater benefit for all.
  • In an ideal world, those with elevated status and authority would have earned it via reliable and consistent public displays of such skill, knowledge and capabilities.
  • In an ideal world, the responsibility and trust associated with status and authority would be a serious yet welcome obligation.

We do not live in an ideal world

:-(

  • We live in a digital world where architecture in some sense defines power and control.
  • So what can we do?

P2P Crypto Messaging

Problem: secure decentralised message delivery to offline peers.

  • Can trusted online friends "pass the message" until delivered?
  • Can this be done in a completely decentralised way?
  • Signalling and discovery are key! How? With a DHT!

Universal DHT

  • A distributed and decentralised k/v store.
  • No single point of failure or control
  • Scales to huge numbers of nodes
  • Lookup is relatively efficient
  • Good handling of fluid network membership
  • Tested in real world (Bittorrent, Freenet etc)

Universal DHT

My current obsession as the drogulus

  • Solves the problem of discoverability and signalling.
  • P4P2P - DHT within DHT (or namespaced / scoped DHT).
